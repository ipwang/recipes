Cream Puffs

This is my recipe for cream puffs! I've broken it up into two major components: the Choux Batter and the Whipped Cream. The directions are also written in shorthand.
I based this recipe heavily on two online recipes (links listed at the bottom!).
I've loved cream puffs since I was a child, and I hope to share this recipe so others can also enjoy them! I've opted for a cream based on whipped cream instead of custard cream for a more airy taste. Feel free to adjust the amount of sugar to your liking UWU
Happy baking!

Choux Batter

Materials and Ingredients

  • pot
  • sieve
  • spatula
  • heat-safe mixing bowl
  • piping bag (alternative: ziploc bag with scissors)
  • parchment paper (alternate: buttered up aluminum foil)
  • baking pan
Ingredient Amount (US) Amount (INTL)
Water 0.423 cup 100 mL
Unsalted butter 1/2 cup 60 g
Salt 1 pinch
Cake flour 1/2 cup 60 g
Eggs 3

NOTE: I get it -- It's a pain to buy cake flour separately. So here's a quick hack to make cake flour from corn starch and flour!
1 cup cake flour = (2 tbsp corn starch) + (1 cup - 2 tbsp flour)
So for this recipe:
1/2 cup cake flour = (1 tbsp corn starch) + (1/2 cup - 1 tbsp flour)

Prepratory Directions

  • preheat the oven to 390 F.
  • sieve the cake flour into a bowl.
  • beat the 3 eggs into a bowl.
  • line the baking pan with parchment paper.
  • place the piping bag into a cup so it is ready to be filled!
  • Tip: use room-temperature eggs and butter.

Instructions

  1. water + butter + salt in pot.
  2. heat medium. heat OFF when butter melted, rolling boil.
  3. add cake flour. stir until even/gooey.
  4. heat medium. stir until thin film forms.
  5. place into bowl. flatten to cool.
  6. mix in eggs slowly in parts. con't until butter drops in "V."
  7. place into piping bag.
  8. pipe onto parchment. use water+finger to flatten peaks.
  9. bake for ~30 min.
  10. cool completely before adding cream.

Stabilized Whipped Cream

Materials and Ingredients

  • small pot
  • stand mixer (alternative: whisk and arm strength!)
  • mixing bowl
  • piping bag (alternative: ziploc bag with scissors)
Ingredient Amount (US)
Cold Water 4 tsp
Unflavored gelatin 1 tsp
Heavy Cream 1 cup
Confectioner's sugar 1/4 cup
Vanilla Extract 1/2 tsp

NOTES: A few drops will do for the vanilla extract; no need to be super accurate on that one.
And again, it's a pain to buy confectioner's sugar separately. You can substitute confectioner's sugar with corn starch and sugar! For this recipe, use a little more than 1 tsp of corn starch and a little less of 1/4 cup sugar -- preferably pulsed/blended/processed. Changing the amount of sugar added affects how sweet the cream is in the end. For a less sweet cream, use less sugar!

Prepratory Directions

  • If you don't have a stand mixer, you may want to start whisking the heavy cream (step 3) before putting heating the gelatin (step 1).

Instructions

  1. sprinkle gelatin over wtaer in pot. let stay for 5 min (no stirring).
  2. heat LOW. stir constantly until gelatin dissolved. set aside.
  3. whisk heavy cream + sugar + vanilla extract until thicc.
  4. slowly add gelatin mixture.
  5. beat until fluffy.
  6. place into piping bag.
  7. pipe into cream puffs (once cooled).
